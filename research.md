## Relevant Industry Standards

- **ACSM Health/Fitness Facility Standards and Guidelines (5th ed.)** – Sets benchmarks for space allocation, equipment safety, environmental controls (humidity 60%, temperature 68–72°F), and emergency preparedness protocols.
- **ACE Industry Guidelines** – American Council on Exercise guidance on instructor qualifications, safe programming, and consumer protection practices.
- **IHRSA Operational Guidelines** – Industry benchmarks for staff-to-member ratios, retention metrics, and financial KPIs used by health club operators worldwide.
- **AED and CPR Requirements** – Most US states require automated external defibrillators on premises and trained first-responder staff; software must track certification expiry.
- **ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)** – Facility access and programme accommodation obligations affecting scheduling and service design.
- **PCI DSS** – Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard applies to all studios processing card transactions; membership billing platforms must maintain compliance.

## Market Research

The fitness studio management software market was valued at approximately USD 1.5 billion in 2024 and is forecast to reach USD 3.5 billion by 2033 at a CAGR near 9.7%. Separately, the gym management software segment grew from USD 2.03 billion in 2025 to roughly USD 2.23 billion in 2026, on a trajectory to USD 4.02 billion by 2032 at a 10.24% CAGR. Cloud-based platforms now account for approximately 57% of software usage, displacing legacy desktop installs as operators demand real-time class occupancy data and mobile instructor access. ACSM's 2026 fitness trends survey identifies HIIT, group training, and wearable tech integration among the top drivers of boutique studio investment—all requiring software to orchestrate waitlists, capacity management, and device data ingestion. North America leads with over 40% market share, though Asia-Pacific is the fastest-growing region as urban fitness culture expands. Consolidation is underway, with ABC Fitness acquiring Glofox and Mindbody extending its marketplace to capture demand from both operators and end consumers.

## AI-Native Opportunity

- **Dynamic class capacity and waitlist optimisation** – AI trained on historical booking and no-show patterns could automatically adjust class caps, trigger targeted waitlist promotions, and predict demand surges to prevent under- and over-booking.
- **Churn prediction and personalised re-engagement** – Member attendance pattern analysis could surface at-risk members weeks before cancellation, enabling timely automated outreach with personalised incentives.
- **Instructor performance and scheduling intelligence** – Correlate instructor assignments with class fill rates, member ratings, and revenue to surface optimal scheduling decisions and flag burnout risk.
- **Retail and merchandise demand forecasting** – AI-powered inventory suggestions for studio retail (apparel, supplements) based on class attendance trends and seasonal data.
- **AI-generated programming templates** – LLM-assisted workout and class programme design that adapts to studio niche, member progression data, and ACSM safety guidelines.
